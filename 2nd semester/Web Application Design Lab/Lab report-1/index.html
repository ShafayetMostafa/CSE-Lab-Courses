RAJSHAHI

Rajshahi is a city in northwestern Bangladesh, known for its historical significance, rich cultural heritage, and scenic beauty. Often referred to as the "Silk City" and the "Education City," it is famous for producing high-quality silk, mangoes, and other agricultural products. Rajshahi is home to several ancient sites, including the Puthia Temple Complex and the ruins of the historic Mahasthangarh. It is also an important educational center, housing Rajshahi University and other institutions. The city lies along the Padma River, adding to its picturesque landscape.
